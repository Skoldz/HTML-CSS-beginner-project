Marie Curie

The first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields

Marie Curie: “Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood” (Colourised black and white photo, Dana Keller/The Maryland Science Centre)

Marie Curie's Life Timeline

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Poland, in what was then the Kingdom of Poland, part of the Russian Empire.
  • 1891 - Moves to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne (University of Paris) because women could not attend higher education institutions in Poland.
  • 1893 - Graduates first in her class in physics. Starts research in an industrial laboratory.
  • 1894 - Meets Pierre Curie, a French physicist, whom she would later marry.
  • 1895 - Marries Pierre Curie and becomes Marie Curie. She starts her doctoral research on uranium rays.
  • 1897 - Gives birth to her first daughter, Irène. Despite her new responsibilities, Curie continues her scientific research.
  • 1903 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics, along with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel, for their work on radioactivity, making her the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.
  • 1906 - Pierre Curie dies in an accident, leaving Marie a widow. She takes over his teaching
  • 1911 - Wins her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for her discovery of the elements radium and polonium. She is the first person and only woman to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.
  • 1914 - The Radium Institute (now the Curie Institute), which she helped found, opens. Its mission is to research radioactivity and treat cancer.
  • 1921 - Visits the United States to raise funds for research on radium.
  • 1934 - Dies from aplastic anemia, believed to be caused by her long-term exposure to radiation, in Passy, France.
  • 1995 - Her remains are transferred to the Panthéon in Paris, in recognition of her contributions to science, making her the first woman to be honored in this way for her own achievements.
